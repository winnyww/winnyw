The T-shirt

The T-shirt

This is a wireless wearable device using neopixel arts. It creates an alternitive way to express happiness during pandemic times when people can't see each other's faces.

2020 has been a year of huge tremble. The COVID-19 wasn't the first global pandemic, and it won't be the last one. Our generation might have to face new "norms" of living in the near future: social distancing rules, remote learning and working, and wear protections gears such as masks. When is the next global catastrophic tremble? How our life will be if one day humans have to cocoon themselves with masks and gloves to survive?

We are already living in one of these realities where seeing each other's face is a luxury and we already lost one essential norm: the ability to communicate with one and another through facial emotional expressions.

During the process, I chose MyoWare Muscle Sensor touching the cheek to detect the movement of smiling. It offers accurate data and by placing it in the right position, I can narrow down the facial movements in order to detect a true smile. For the neopixel panle, I designed three pattern arts of different meanings:

the pattern of the yellow arrow continues to loop non-stop, representing the time passing

Pink pattern only appears when a smile is detected, expressing the idea that the person is having a good time

Blue pattern will appear once the user finishes using. The number of ellipses is proportional to the amount of time spent on smiling
