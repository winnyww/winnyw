Disability Studies

This is a project for an experiential learning NYU class about the world of disability. As a member of a team including a guest consultant living with an impairment, I have explored my consultant's interests, abilities, and desires and portrayed him with narrative digital storytelling.

HeartShare is a New York based organization which offers day service to my consultant Alfonso. NYU offers a disability studies class every semester, consultants from HeartShare sit in the same classroom with NYU students and together, they develop a film project and close friendship.

When New York locked down the city due to COVID-19, HeartShare closed its program, NYU also shut down the campuses. So after half a semester, my consultant Alfonso and I lost contact with each other. When I finally found him via his brother's phone, I realized the situation hasn't been easy for him: without the program, he basically stayed at home all the time alone. The worse thing is that his family couldn't get a new cable for their internet and TV. That's why I couldn't reach out to him at the beginning when he was all alone in a dark world.

source from HeartShare.org


The only way we could stay in touch is through his brother's phone. So I started to diary our phone dialogue every evening. We were both in quarentine and we shared how our days went, our hobbies and so on. I collected a lot of materials from our conversation and made this film project.

Alfonso got new cable in a few weeks and we still make video calls even after our class is over.
